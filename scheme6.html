Stand-Up India

About the Scheme

Launched on April 5, 2016, the Stand-Up India scheme is a flagship initiative of the Government of India aimed at fostering entrepreneurship among women and SC/ST communities. The scheme facilitates bank loans ranging from ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore to promote greenfield ventures—first-time business projects in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors.

Stand-Up India ensures inclusive access to institutional credit, enabling underrepresented entrepreneurs to enter the formal economy with confidence. The scheme mandates that each bank branch supports at least one SC/ST and one woman entrepreneur.

By July 2021, over 1.16 lakh loans had been sanctioned under the scheme, with 81% of the beneficiaries being women entrepreneurs, reflecting its strong emphasis on gender and social equity in entrepreneurship.

Key Features of Stand-Up India:

  • Focus on greenfield projects by first-time entrepreneurs.
  • Target group: SC/ST and women entrepreneurs aged 18 and above.
  • Loan range: ₹10 lakh to ₹1 crore (composite loan for term loan and working capital).
  • Repayment period: Up to 7 years with a moratorium of up to 18 months.
  • Offers handholding support through SIDBI and other facilitation agencies.
